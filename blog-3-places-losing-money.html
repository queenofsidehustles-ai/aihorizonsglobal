The 3 Places Your Business Is Quietly Losing Time and Money

By Monica Lewis · July 11, 2026 · 5 min read

Here's something 20 years of walking into other people's operations taught me: the biggest leaks are almost never where people think they are. Owners look for one dramatic problem — a bad hire, a slow month, a broken tool. But real waste rarely announces itself. It hides in plain sight, tucked into the normal rhythm of the workday where nobody thinks to look.

If you want to find the money and time your business is losing right now, start with these three places.

1. The handoffs

Work almost never dies while one person is doing it. It dies in the space between people — when a job passes from sales to operations, from one teammate to another, from a manager's decision to the person who has to carry it out.

That gap is where things get dropped, delayed, or redone. A quote sits in someone's inbox for two days. A booking gets entered twice. A customer question bounces between three people before anyone answers it. None of it feels like a crisis, so nobody fixes it. But add up a dozen small handoff delays a week, every week, and you're looking at real money.

Watch what happens to a task the moment it leaves one person's hands. That's where your business bleeds.

2. "We've always done it this way"

Every business is carrying steps that made sense once and stopped making sense years ago. A form that gets filled out and never read. A weekly report nobody uses. An approval that adds two days and zero value.

These survive not because they work, but because no one has permission to question them. The person who created the step is gone; the people doing it assume it matters. When I sit with a team and ask a simple question — "what would break if we just stopped doing this?" — the honest answer is often "nothing." That's a leak you can close this week.

3. The work nobody owns

Ask a team who is responsible for following up on a stalled job, and watch the room go quiet. The most expensive work in any organization is the work that lives in the cracks — important enough to matter, but not clearly owned by anyone.

When something belongs to "everyone," it belongs to no one. It's the lead that never got called back. The invoice that never went out. The customer who slipped away because three people each assumed someone else had it. Accountability isn't about blame — it's about clarity. The moment one name is attached to a thing, it stops falling through the floor.

How to find yours this week

You don't need a consultant to start. Pick one job your business does often — a booking, a quote, an onboarding — and follow a single one all the way through, start to finish. Write down every hand it passes through and every place it waits. The waiting is your waste. The waiting is where AI and better process pay for themselves.

Here's the good news: once you can see the leaks, most are surprisingly cheap to fix. And a lot of them are exactly the kind of repetitive, in-between work that AI handles beautifully — once the process underneath it is sound.
