Before You Buy Another AI Tool, Fix This First

By Monica Lewis · July 11, 2026 · 4 min read

There's a quiet panic in a lot of businesses right now. Everyone feels like they're behind on AI, so they do the natural thing: they go buy a tool. A chatbot. An automation app. A shiny subscription somebody swore by on LinkedIn. Three months later, most of those tools are sitting unused — an expensive reminder that something didn't work.

Here's the truth I've watched play out for two decades, long before AI: a tool can't fix a broken process. It can only make a broken process run faster.

If your workflow is a leaky bucket, AI just helps you pour water in quicker. You still lose it out the bottom — you just lose it faster.

Why smart businesses waste money on AI

It's not the tool's fault, and it's not yours. It's the order of operations. Most AI implementations fail for the same reason most software rollouts have always failed: the technology gets dropped on top of a workflow nobody mapped, owned by no one, that people were already working around.

So the AI automates the confusion. It speeds up a handoff that shouldn't exist. It generates content for a follow-up process that has no follow-up. The mess is now faster and harder to see — and everyone quietly goes back to the old way.

The smarter first move isn't a tool

Before you spend another dollar on AI, answer one question: where, exactly, does work stall in your business today? Not the tool you want — the problem you have. Where do things wait? What gets done by hand that shouldn't? Where does work fall through the cracks?

When you can name the real bottleneck, the right AI becomes obvious — and it usually costs far less than the pile of tools you'd have bought guessing. That's the whole idea behind how we work: we start with your people and their workflows, not with a product we're trying to sell you. Fix the process first, then let AI make the good process effortless.

A 10-minute gut check

Ask your team one question this week: "What's the most annoying, repetitive thing you do that you're sure a computer should be handling by now?" Their answers are a map. They'll point you straight at the places where AI creates real value — because it's solving a problem your people actually feel, not one a salesperson invented.

Do that, and you'll never buy a tool that gathers dust again. You'll buy the one thing your business was quietly asking for.
